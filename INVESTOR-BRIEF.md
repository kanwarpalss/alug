# alug — investor brief

**Watch straps and watch-culture goods for India's mechanical watch boom.**
*alug* = Hindi *alag* (different) + the watch **lug**, where a strap meets a watch.

> [!NOTE]
> Prepared July 2026. Market figures are sourced (links at end); unit economics are founder estimates and labelled as such. Working prototype of the store exists and is demo-ready.

---

## 1. Thesis in three sentences

India is the **fastest-growing Swiss watch market in the world**, and every one of those watches is sold with exactly one strap. A strap wardrobe (₹2,000–5,600 per strap) is the cheapest way to "own four watches", yet no Indian brand serves the enthusiast who knows what a lug width is. alug is a design-led, connoisseur-grade strap and watch-culture brand built for exactly that buyer — capped at **$150K** to prove or kill the thesis inside 18 months.

## 2. The macro tailwind (sourced)

| Signal | Number |
|---|---|
| Swiss watch export growth to India, 2025 vs 2023 | **+35.2%** (world's fastest; global market shrank 1.7%) |
| Growth in 2024 alone | +25.2% |
| India–EFTA trade deal (in force Oct 2025) | Watch import duty **22% → 0%** in equal steps by 2031 |
| Dollar-millionaires in India (2024) | **917,000**, projected +50% by 2028 |
| Indian consumers who see a watch as an investment | **28% — highest in the world** |
| Retail expansion | Ethos: 70+ stores incl. 2,000 m² "City of Time"; Rolex/PP/AP/IWC opening monobrand boutiques; Bengaluru & Hyderabad next wave |

**Why straps specifically:** each imported watch is a one-time purchase; straps are the repeatable, giftable, seasonal attach. Quick-release spring bars (now standard) turned strap-changing from a watchmaker's job into an 8-second habit.

## 3. The customer

Owner of a **₹70,000+ ($800+) mechanical watch** — Omega, Longines, Tudor, TAG Heuer, Rado, Breitling, and the enthusiast Seiko tier. We mapped the **top 20 references seen on serious Indian wrists** with lug widths and strap pairings (productised as the "Shop by Watch" page). For a person who spent ₹4 lakh on a Seamaster, a ₹4,500 strap is a 1% accessory — the mental math of a phone case, with the emotion of a new watch.

Psychographics: self-purchasers, Instagram-native, community-driven (watch meetups in Bombay/Bangalore/Delhi), allergic to "luxury replica" aesthetics, reward honesty and specification depth.

## 4. Product

| Line | Price band | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Straps — leather, suede, FKM rubber, sailcloth, NATO | ₹2,000–5,600 | Core revenue |
| **The 21 Club** — true 21mm straps | ₹4,900 | Wedge product: HydroConquest & Submariner owners are ignored by the industry's even-numbers bias |
| Framed watch art (original retro-style, no trademarks) | ₹4,200–5,400 | Margin-rich, giftable, brand-builder |
| Paper goods (collector notebooks/planners) | ₹900–1,400 | Entry price point, gifting, humour |

Differentiators no Indian competitor has: **lug-width-first navigation, shop-by-watch-model, honest-fit warnings** (we tell Santos owners our straps won't fit), editorial voice ("NO APPLE WATCHES HERE"), and design by a founder who is the customer.

## 5. Competition

| Segment | Players | Gap alug exploits |
|---|---|---|
| Indian D2C leather | Khisa, Indian Leather Craft, HelloEDC, Sirphire | Generic "premium leather" positioning; no watch-enthusiast depth, no odd widths, no culture |
| Indian mass-market | Invella, Amazon.in / Flipkart flood | ₹300–800 commodity; actively insults a ₹4L watch |
| Global enthusiast brands | Delugs (SG), Barton, Crown & Buckle (US) | The proof the model works — but India delivery = duties on arrival, 1–4 week waits, painful returns |
| Indian B2B manufacturing | VBL Innovations, Bengaluru (25 yrs, ~4M straps/yr) | Not a competitor — a **potential white-label partner** enabling "Made in India" without owning a factory |

> [!TIP]
> Delugs is the template: enthusiast-grade straps, content-led, global cult following. alug is that playbook executed **inside** the world's fastest-growing watch market, in rupees, with 2-day domestic shipping.

## 7. Go-to-market

**Phase 1 (months 1–4): credibility.** Instagram content engine (strap-pairing reels per watch model), presence at Bombay/Bangalore/Delhi watch meetups, seeding with 20–30 known Indian collectors. Cities in that order — density of the top-20 watch models follows boutique density.
**Phase 2 (months 4–9): conversion.** D2C site live with Razorpay + COD, shop-by-watch SEO pages ("best strap for Tudor Black Bay 58 India"), the 21 Club as PR hook.
**Phase 3 (months 9–18): expansion.** Marketplace listings (Amazon premium, Tata CLiQ Luxury), pop-ups with independent watch retailers, art/paper line into design stores.

## 10. Honest risks

1. **Niche depth unknown.** India's enthusiast base is growing fast but current spend on aftermarket straps is unproven — that's precisely what the $150K buys: an answer.
2. **Lug-width accuracy is brand-critical.** One wrong recommendation kills trust; all published specs get verified against manufacturer data before launch (in progress).
3. **Sourcing quality variance.** Mitigated by dual-track sampling (China + VBL Bengaluru) and small first runs.
4. **IP care on art line.** All retro art is original reimagining — no brand logos or trademark reproductions.
5. **Copycats.** The moat is taste, editorial voice and community trust — defensible only through consistency, not patents. We know this.
6. **Founder concentration.** Solo founder doing design + curation; part-time ops hire budgeted from month 4.
